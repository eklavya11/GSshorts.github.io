Constitutional Amendments

Under Article 368, of the constituition the Parliament has the power of amending the constitution.The constitution can be amended by simple majority in matters relating to citizenship, alteration of boundaries of existing states etc.

Who gave the idea of constitution?

Manabendra nath roy gave the idea of constitution.He was a radical activist as well as political theorist.He was a founder of Communist party of India.He is also known as the father of communism in India.

Regulating act of 1773

It was passed by the Parliament of Britain to regulate the affairs of East India Company officialy.This act also designated Governor of Bengal as the Governor-general of Bengal who has the authority over the Presidencies of Bengal, Madras, Calcutta.The first such Governer general was Warren Hastings.

Govt of India Act, 1858

This act transfered the power from East India Company to British Crown.The dual system of government came to an end.The Governor-general was made the viceroy of India.Lord canning became the first Viceroy of India.The post of Secretary of State was also established.

What is bicameralism?

In Indian parliament we have 2 houses, Lok sabha and Rajya sabha.Also in state governments we have 2 houses in 6 states(Bihar, Andhra Pradesh, karnataka, Maharashtra, Uttar Pradesh and Telangana).This practice is known as Bicameralism.

Mountbatten Plan

The partition plan was put forth by Lord Mountbatten.This plan was accepted by both Congress and Muslim League.Immediate effect was given to plan by enacting the Indian Independence Act , 1947 that ended British rule in India and declared India as an Independent state from 15th August, 1947.

Drafting Committee

The constitution assembly appointed a Drafting committee on 29th August,1947.Dr BR Ambedkar who was the chairman of drafting committee submitted the first draft of Constitution of India to the President of assembly on 21st feb, 1948.

Enactment and Enforcement of Constitution.

On 26th Nov,1949, constitution was adopted, containing a preamble and 395 Articles, 22 parts and 8 schedules.Now it has 448 Articles, 25 parts and 12 schedules. The constitution came into force on 26th January 1950.

First Linguistic State

In oct, 1953, the Government of India was forced to create First Linguistic state, known as Andhra Pradesh, by separating Telugu speaking area from Madras presidency.Kurnool was first capital of Andhra Pradesh.
